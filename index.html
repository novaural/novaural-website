The Challenge

Hearing Aid Fitting Is Still
Trial and Error

Despite advances in hearing aid technology, the fitting process itself has barely changed. Three key problems persist across the industry.

🔄

Multiple Follow‑Up Visits

Patients return repeatedly because fittings are adjusted based on vague feedback — "it sounds too tinny" or "I can't hear in restaurants". Each visit costs time and money with no guarantee of improvement.

📉

No Objective Measurement

Clinicians lack tools that quantify how well a patient actually discriminates speech sounds after fitting. Without data, adjustments are educated guesses at best.

🚪

High Abandonment Rates

Industry data shows significant hearing aid abandonment. Patients who don't experience clear improvement quickly are likely to stop using their devices altogether.

Our Technology

Introducing ReFittingLab

A working software platform that gives clinicians the data they need to optimise fittings — quantitatively. Currently operational and seeking clinical validation partners.

🎤

Phonemic Discrimination Testing

Scientifically validated minimal pairs test how patients perceive individual speech contrasts (e.g., /s/ vs /θ/, /p/ vs /b/) — revealing precisely where the fitting falls short.

🧮

Quantitative Adjustment Engine

Our patented algorithm translates phonemic errors into specific, per-frequency gain adjustments — replacing guesswork with evidence-based recommendations.

📊

Multi‑Level Protocol

Tests at 50, 65, and 80 dB SPL verify audibility, intelligibility, and comfort in a single structured session covering real-world listening conditions.

Multilingual Design

The Process

How ReFittingLab Works

A structured, evidence-based workflow that integrates seamlessly into existing clinical practice.

1

Input Patient Profile

Enter audiogram, current gains, and clinical context. The system configures the optimal test protocol automatically.

2

Administer Tests

Guided phoneme discrimination testing at multiple levels, with speech-in-noise and directionality verification.

3

Analyse Results

The algorithm maps phonemic errors to frequency bands and calculates precise gain adjustments for each channel.

4

Apply & Verify

Apply recommendations in your fitting software, then re-test to confirm measurable improvement in discrimination.
