Where Does ReFittingLab Fit
in Your Clinical Workflow?

Current best practice excels at measuring what reaches the ear — REM, REAR, audiogram targets. But it has no standardised way to measure what the patient actually perceives and discriminates after fitting. That's the gap ReFittingLab fills.

The Problem

The Fine-Tuning Gap

After applying a prescriptive formula and verifying with REM, the clinician enters the fine-tuning phase — and suddenly loses access to objective data.

❌ What happens today during fine-tuning

  • "How does it sound?" — Clinicians ask subjective questions with no quantifiable answers
  • Trial and error — Adjust gains, send patient home, wait for next appointment
  • 3–5 return visits — Each one essentially repeating the same subjective loop
  • No phoneme-level data — If the patient says "I can't understand speech," the clinician doesn't know which frequencies are causing the issue
  • Patient frustration — 30% of hearing aid users abandon their devices within 12 months

✅ What changes with ReFittingLab

  • Objective phoneme discrimination scores — Per-contrast, per-frequency, quantified
  • Targeted adjustments — The algorithm tells you exactly which bands need +1, +2, +3 dB
  • Measurable improvement — Re-test immediately to confirm the adjustment worked
  • Fewer visits — More gets resolved in the first session because you have data, not guesses
  • Clinical documentation — Every decision is evidence-based and auditable

The verification gap in current practice

Pre-fitting has excellent objective tools: audiogram, REM/REAR, RECD, prescriptive formulas (NAL-NL2, DSLv5, manufacturer-based).
Post-fitting fine-tuning has almost none. The clinician adjusts based on subjective patient reports and their own experience — with no way to measure if a specific phoneme pair (/s/ vs /θ/, /p/ vs /b/) improved after a gain change.

ReFittingLab bridges this gap by providing a structured, quantitative verification protocol that maps phoneme discrimination performance directly to frequency-band gain adjustments.

Clinical Context

Where ReFittingLab Fits in Your Workflow

ReFittingLab does not replace your fitting software or prescriptive formula. It enters the workflow after the initial fitting, exactly where objective data disappears.

1

Audiogram & Patient History

Standard audiometric evaluation: air/bone conduction, UCL, speech recognition threshold. This is your baseline.

2

Prescriptive Formula

Apply your formula of choice — NAL-NL2, DSLv5.0, or a manufacturer-based formula (Phonak APD, Oticon VAC+, etc.) — to generate initial gain targets.

3

Initial Fitting in Manufacturer Software

Program the hearing aids in Phonak Target, Oticon Genie 2, ReSound Smart Fit, Signia Connexx, Widex Compass GPS, or any other fitting software. Configure coupling, feedback management, features.

4

Real-Ear Measurement (REM/REAR)

Verify that the acoustic output at the eardrum matches prescriptive targets. This confirms the physical delivery of sound — but not the perceptual outcome.

★

ReFittingLab — Phoneme Discrimination Verification

This is where ReFittingLab enters. The system evaluates the patient's ability to discriminate speech sounds across frequency bands — using minimal pairs, word lists, and speech-in-noise — and outputs specific, per-band gain adjustments (ΔG50, ΔG65, ΔG80) to resolve measured deficits.

The clinician then applies these adjustments in their fitting software and re-tests to confirm measurable improvement. No guesswork. No waiting for the next appointment.

6

Fine-Tuning with Data

Apply ReFittingLab's recommendations in your fitting software. Adjust G65 for conversational speech, G50 for soft sounds, G80 for loud environments — each adjustment mapped to a specific phoneme discrimination deficit.

7

Verified Outcome & Discharge

Re-test with ReFittingLab to confirm improvement. Document results. Discharge with confidence — or schedule follow-up only if objective data warrants it.

The Process — In Detail

What Happens During a ReFittingLab Session

A complete verification session takes 20–30 minutes and produces actionable, per-band gain adjustment recommendations.

STEP 1

Input Patient Profile

Enter the audiogram (air/bone conduction, UCL), current gain tables (G50/G65/G80/CR from your fitting software), and clinical context: experience level, tinnitus, hyperacusis, coupling type.

~2 min Pre-session
STEP 2

Automatic Test Selection

Based on the audiometric profile, the system selects which phoneme contrasts to prioritise. Steep high-frequency loss? Prioritise fricatives (/s/ vs /θ/). Flat moderate loss? Include stops and nasals. Severe HF loss? Flag for frequency lowering verification.

Automatic Profile-driven
STEP 3

Phoneme Discrimination Testing

Calibrated minimal pair stimuli are presented at 65 dB SPL (conversational level). The patient identifies what they hear — casa or caza? peso or beso? Each error maps to a specific frequency band and distinctive feature.

~12 min 65 dB SPL 12 contrasts
STEP 4

Multi-Level Testing

Optionally extend to 50 dB (audibility of soft sounds) and 80 dB (comfort and tolerance). This maps to G50, G65, and G80 adjustments across the full dynamic range — not just conversational level.

~8 min 50/65/80 dB
STEP 5

Speech-in-Noise Verification

Sentences are presented with calibrated noise (ICRA/ISTS) at multiple signal-to-noise ratios (+5, 0, −5 dB). Calculates the patient's SNR loss — how many extra dB of signal they need compared to a normal listener.

~6 min 3 SNR levels
STEP 6

Analysis & Recommendations

The algorithm maps each phoneme error to its acoustic correlate (distinctive feature → frequency band), then calculates the gain delta needed to resolve the deficit. Output: prioritised per-band ΔG50/ΔG65/ΔG80 and compression ratio adjustments.

Instant 20 bands 170 Hz – 9.6 kHz
STEP 7

Apply in Your Fitting Software

Take the recommendations and apply them directly in Phonak Target, Oticon Genie 2, ReSound Smart Fit, or whichever fitting software you use. ReFittingLab tells you what to change and why — you stay in control.

~3 min Any manufacturer
STEP 8

Re-Test & Verify

Re-administer the tests that showed deficits. If the adjustment was correct, discrimination scores improve measurably (using Thornton-Raffin critical difference tables to confirm statistical significance). Document and discharge — or iterate.

~5 min Evidence-based
Under the Hood

From Phoneme Error to Gain Adjustment

The core algorithm translates a perceptual error into a precise, band-specific gain change. Here's the pipeline:

Phoneme Error

"casa" heard as "caza"

→
Distinctive Feature

[+strident] vs [−strident]

→
Frequency Band

4–8 kHz

→
Gain Delta

G65 +3 dB @ 4 kHz

This is not a generic "boost highs if speech is unclear" approach. Each phoneme contrast maps to a specific set of distinctive features (Jakobson-Halle-Fant framework), which in turn correspond to specific frequency bands. The algorithm considers the patient's audiogram, current gains, dynamic range, and clinical profile to calculate a bounded, safe adjustment — typically 1–3 dB per band.

Clinical Impact

Why Objective Fine-Tuning Data Matters

📊

From Subjective to Objective

Instead of "How does it sound?", you get "/s/ vs /θ/ discrimination: 68% → needs +3 dB at 4 kHz". Every adjustment has a reason. Every reason has data.

🎯

Targeted, Not Global

ReFittingLab doesn't suggest "boost highs." It identifies which frequency band is causing which phoneme confusion, and calculates the minimum effective adjustment — typically 1–3 dB.

🔄

Immediate Verification Loop

Adjust → Re-test → Confirm. In the same session. No more "come back in two weeks and tell me how it went." Statistically significant improvement is verified on the spot.

📋

Clinical Documentation

Every test result, every recommendation, every re-test is recorded. Useful for clinical governance, audits (NHS/CQC), and longitudinal tracking of patient outcomes.

⏱️

More Done in Fewer Visits

Data-driven adjustments resolve more issues in the first fitting session. The traditional 3–5 visit cycle compresses — better outcomes, happier patients, more efficient clinics.

Language-Independent Architecture
